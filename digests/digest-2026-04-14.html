Top 5 Papers

#1
BREAKWATER: Encorafenib, Cetuximab, and mFOLFOX6 in BRAF V600E-Mutated Metastatic CRC
Source: New England Journal of Medicine  |  Authors: Elez E et al.  |  Published: April 2026
Score: 9.5/10 — NEJM Phase III; FDA-approved Feb 2026; new SOC for BRAF V600E mCRC with PFS 12.8 vs 7.1 months (HR 0.53)
This international Phase III trial demonstrated that encorafenib plus cetuximab plus mFOLFOX6 significantly improves progression-free survival compared to standard chemotherapy in BRAF V600E-mutant metastatic colorectal cancer (12.8 vs 7.1 months; HR 0.53; P<0.001). Interim overall survival analysis showed a median of 30.3 vs 15.1 months (HR 0.49). The triplet regimen received FDA approval on February 24, 2026, establishing a new standard of care for this molecularly-defined subgroup (~8-10% of mCRC patients).
Post angle: BREAKWATER in NEJM: encorafenib-based triplet doubles survival in BRAF V600E mCRC (30 vs 15 months). From worst prognosis to a treatable molecular subtype. Test early, treat precisely. #ColorectalCancer #BRAFV600E #PrecisionOncology #GIOncology
#2
HERIZON-GEA-01: Zanidatamab + Chemotherapy ± Tislelizumab in First-Line HER2+ Gastroesophageal Adenocarcinoma
Source: Journal of Clinical Oncology / ASCO GI 2026 (LBA)  |  Authors: Elimova E et al.  |  Published: January 2026 (ASCO GI 2026)
Score: 9.4/10 — Phase III LBA at ASCO GI; zanidatamab bispecific HER2 targeting; median OS >26.4 months; potential new 1L standard
This global Phase III trial compared zanidatamab, a bispecific HER2-targeted antibody, plus chemotherapy with or without tislelizumab versus trastuzumab plus chemotherapy in HER2-positive metastatic gastroesophageal adenocarcinoma. The zanidatamab arm achieved median PFS of 12.4 vs 8.1 months and median OS exceeding 26.4 months vs 19.2 months for trastuzumab/chemo. These results position zanidatamab as a potential new standard-of-care HER2-targeting agent in the first-line setting.
Post angle: HERIZON-GEA-01: bispecific HER2 targeting with zanidatamab shows >7-month OS advantage over trastuzumab in first-line HER2+ gastric cancer. The bispecific antibody era has arrived. #GastricCancer #HER2 #ASCOGI2026 #GIOncology
#3
COMMIT: Atezolizumab + mFOLFOX6/Bevacizumab vs Atezolizumab Monotherapy in First-Line dMMR/MSI-H mCRC
Source: Journal of Clinical Oncology / ASCO GI 2026  |  Authors: Rocha Lima CMS et al.  |  Published: January 2026 (ASCO GI 2026)
Score: 9.2/10 — Phase III at ASCO GI; striking PFS benefit (24.5 vs 5.3 months; HR 0.44) in biomarker-selected dMMR/MSI-H population
The COMMIT Phase III trial compared the triplet of mFOLFOX6/bevacizumab/atezolizumab versus atezolizumab monotherapy in first-line dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal cancer. The triplet showed dramatically better PFS (24.5 vs 5.3 months; HR 0.439; P=0.01) and ORR (86.1% vs 46%, with CR 36.1% vs 18.9%). No OS difference was observed (HR 1.04). These results challenge the IO-monotherapy paradigm in dMMR mCRC, raising important questions about optimal first-line strategy.
Post angle: COMMIT in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC: adding chemo+bev to atezolizumab boosts ORR to 86% and PFS from 5 to 25 months. But no OS benefit. Does response depth matter if survival is the same? A key debate. #ColorectalCancer #MSI #Immunotherapy #ASCOGI2026
#4
PELICAN Trial: Radiofrequency Ablation + Chemotherapy vs Chemotherapy Alone in Locally Advanced Pancreatic Cancer
Source: Journal of Clinical Oncology  |  Authors: Seelen LWF et al.  |  Published: April 2026
Score: 8.1/10 — Phase III RCT; important negative trial — RFA did not improve OS and worsened quality of life in LAPC
This international randomized Phase III trial compared radiofrequency ablation plus chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone in patients with locally advanced pancreatic cancer who had stable disease after 2 months of induction chemotherapy. RFA did not improve overall survival and was associated with significantly worse quality of life. This important negative trial prevents unnecessary procedural intervention and will influence treatment algorithms for LAPC management.
Post angle: PELICAN: RFA doesn't improve outcomes in locally advanced pancreatic cancer and worsens QOL. Negative trials matter — they protect patients from futile interventions. #PancreaticCancer #ClinicalTrials #GIOncology
#5
GRAPE: AI-Based Large-Scale Screening of Gastric Cancer from Non-Contrast CT Imaging
Source: Nature Medicine  |  Authors: Yang Y et al.  |  Published: 2025 (ongoing clinical discussion 2026)
Score: 8.8/10 — Nature Medicine; AI outperforms radiologists (+21.8% sensitivity); paradigm shift for non-invasive gastric cancer screening
GRAPE (Gastric Cancer Risk Assessment with AI) is a deep learning algorithm that identifies gastric cancer from routine non-contrast CT scans with AUC 0.970 (internal) and 0.927 (external validation). In real-world screening, it detected gastric cancer with 21.8% higher sensitivity and 14.0% higher specificity compared to radiologists, including 23-27% early-stage cases. This approach leverages widely available imaging, potentially enabling non-invasive population-scale screening without dedicated endoscopy.
Post angle: AI reads routine CT scans to detect gastric cancer — outperforming radiologists by 22% sensitivity. GRAPE (Nature Medicine) could transform mass screening in high-incidence regions. #AI #GastricCancer #EarlyDetection #DigitalOncology

Additional Papers of Interest

    NEJM (June 2025) — Janjigian YY et al. — Phase III: perioperative durvalumab + FLOT improved EFS (median NR vs 32.8 mo); ~70% alive at 3 years; FDA-approved.
  2. SCOT Trial Final Results: 3 vs 6 Months Adjuvant Chemotherapy in CRC
    Journal of Clinical Oncology (January 2026) — Kerr RS et al. — Phase III noninferiority: 3-month adjuvant chemo noninferior to 6 months (5-year OS 82.4% both arms) with better QOL.
  3. Tislelizumab + Chemo in Gastric Cancer: Long-Term RATIONALE-305 Follow-Up
    Advances in Therapy (January 2026) — Qiu MZ et al. — 3-year follow-up confirms durable benefit of tislelizumab + chemo in first-line advanced gastric/GEJ cancer.
  4. Low-Dose Aspirin for PI3K-Altered Localized CRC (ALASCCA Trial)
    NEJM (September 2025) — Hua X et al. — RCT: aspirin 160 mg daily for 3 years reduced 3-year recurrence from 14.1% to 7.7% (HR 0.49) in PI3K-altered CRC.
