🔥 #1 Lymph Node Dissection in Esophageal Cancer pCR (Annals of Surgical Oncology) • Meta-analysis: 2,578 pCR patients across 8 studies • Adequate LND → HR 0.70 for OS (p<0.001) • No extra benefit beyond 40 lymph nodes • Key caveat: benefit seen in adenocarcinoma + Western patients • No significant OS benefit in SCC or Eastern populations This is why biology trumps one-size-fits-all surgery. 🎯 #3 Completing Perioperative Chemo After Gastrectomy (World J Surg Oncol) • 145 total gastrectomy patients • Completing perioperative chemo vs neoadjuvant alone: HR 0.20 (p=0.001) • That's an 80% reduction in 1-year mortality • BUT 55% of patients never finished the adjuvant phase • Main barriers: patient refusal + medical contraindications The biggest gap in gastric cancer outcomes isn't drug discovery — it's treatment completion. We must do better at supporting patients through the full course. #GastricCancer #SurgOnc #QualityOfCare #GIOnc https://doi.org/10.1186/s12957-026-04364-w
